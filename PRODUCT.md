# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Casual visitors — anyone who lands on the site looking for a few minutes of fun. No gaming background required. They might be taking a quick break, killing time, or just exploring. The context is low-stakes: a browser tab, a spare moment, no pressure to commit or sign up.

## Product Purpose

A personal game hub hosting a small collection of browser-based mini-games. Each game is immediately playable, no install or login required. The site exists to be genuinely fun — a small, well-made corner of the web that feels good to visit and play in.

## Brand Personality

Clean · Playful · Confident

Voice is understated and warm. The site doesn't hype itself — it lets the games speak. Think Neal.fun: quiet confidence, no cringe, fun without spectacle. Personality comes through craft and small details, not loudness or decoration.

## Anti-references

- **AI slop** — generic AI-generated layouts, cream backgrounds, pastel card grids, meaningless gradients. Anything that looks templated.
- **Cheap mobile-ad games** — flashy banners, fake progress bars, aggressive CTAs, visual noise.
- **Dark gamer cliché** — neon-on-black aggression, dragon aesthetic, toxic energy, overwrought effects.
- **Kids educational site** — chunky primary-color fonts, cartoon mascots, patronizing UX.

## Design Principles

1. **Quiet confidence** — the UI trusts itself and the games. No selling, no hype, no decorative inflation. It just is.
2. **Games first** — each game gets its own visual identity. The hub defers. The nav disappears when you're playing.
3. **Fun through craft, not spectacle** — playfulness lives in good motion, sharp details, and moments of delight — not in loud colors or effects.
4. **Anyone can play** — no gaming vocabulary, no gatekeeping. If you can click, you can play.
5. **Handcrafted, not generated** — every element should feel like a person made a decision about it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG level required. Aim for readable contrast on all text, keyboard navigability for game controls, and reduced-motion support on non-critical animations.
